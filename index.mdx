## How it works

SlideMenu wraps your main content and menu content in a single container. The main view slides aside to reveal the menu beneath, driven by an edge-swipe gesture or a `SlideMenuButton` placed anywhere in your view hierarchy. All appearance settings are passed through the SwiftUI environment, so you can apply modifiers at any level.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the package">
    Add the SlideMenu Swift package to your Xcode project from `https://github.com/matteozappia/SlideMenu.git`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wrap your root view">
    Replace your root view with `SlideMenu`, providing your main content and menu content as trailing closures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a toggle button">
    Drop a `SlideMenuButton` anywhere inside your main content — it picks up the toggle action from the environment automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize appearance">
    Apply `.slideMenuWidth()`, `.slideMenuBackground()`, `.slideMenuAnimation()`, and other modifiers to tailor the look and feel.
  </Step>
</Steps>
